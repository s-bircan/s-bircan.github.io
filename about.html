Research

I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of Sociology at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My research focuses on the intersection of care work, digitalization, and globalization, with particular emphasis on medical sociology, political sociology, and organizational studies.

My current research examines the global flow of digital health technologies, investigating the key actors who initiate this flow and how sociocultural contexts influence their implementation and utilization in diverse hospital settings. My dissertation explores two parallel transformations that the Turkish healthcare system is undergoing — privatization and digitalization — and how these phenomena intersect in healthcare delivery and reshape the nature of care work.

My work draws on qualitative and ethnographic fieldwork with nurses and healthcare professionals in Turkey. I am particularly interested in how frontline workers navigate institutional change, construct moral frameworks around "good care," and engage in everyday forms of resistance within hyper-turbulent organizational settings.

I hold a Master of Arts in Sociology from Middle East Technical University (METU) in Turkey, where my thesis examined transformations in nurses' occupational experiences within the Turkish healthcare system over a 30-year period. This foundational work led to my ongoing research into the structural forces — privatization, digitalization, and global health technology transfer — that reshape care relationships and professional identity.

My research has been published in New Perspectives on Turkey, Sociology of Health & Illness, and Social Movement Studies. I have presented my work at major venues including the American Sociological Association Annual Conference, the British Sociological Association Annual Meeting, and the Eastern and Midwest Sociological Society meetings.

I am a Fulbright Scholar and have received multiple fellowships and awards from the University of Illinois, including the Vivian Lin Thurston Fellowship and the Bastian Summer Fellowship. I have taught courses in global health, sociology of gender, and introductory sociology, and have been recognized with the Excellence in Teaching award across multiple semesters.
